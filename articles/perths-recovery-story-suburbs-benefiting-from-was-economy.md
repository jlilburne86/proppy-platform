# Perth’s Recovery Story: Suburbs Benefiting from WA’s Economy

WA’s economy has supported a sustained improvement in rental demand and buyer interest across selected Perth metros. Here’s where the data points — and what to watch.

![Perth signals](/assets/screenshots/technology.png)
![Compare suburbs](/assets/screenshots/how-it-works.png)
![Portfolio by state](/assets/screenshots/homepage-9.png)
![Heatmap view](/assets/screenshots/platform-screenshot.png)

## Market Context
- Vacancy in several inner‑mid rings has remained tight; rents outpaced national averages in select suburbs.

## Key Drivers
- Employment centres, net migration, and relatively affordable price points versus east coast capitals.

## What the Data Shows
- Suburb clusters with low vacancy and stable days‑on‑market signal resilient demand.

## Expert Insight
- Focus on quality stock and micro‑locations near transport and amenities; avoid compromised streets.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: verify rental appraisals; avoid over‑capitalising.
- Experienced: measure risk via pipeline checks and local overlays.

## Risks & Considerations
- Overpaying during competitive phases; construction capacity and cost risk.

## Key Takeaways
- Let fundamentals and micro‑location drive selection; don’t chase headlines.
- Consider diversification across WA corridors with distinct demand drivers.

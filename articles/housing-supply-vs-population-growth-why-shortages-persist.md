# Housing Supply vs Population Growth: Why Shortages Persist

Approvals and completions have struggled to keep pace with population growth in select states. This article summarises the data and outlines what investors should monitor.

![Approvals vs completions](/assets/screenshots/technology.png)
![Population flows](/assets/screenshots/platform-screenshot.png)
![Pipeline and delivery risk](/assets/screenshots/how-it-works.png)
![Market pressure dashboard](/assets/screenshots/homepage-9.png)

## Market Context
- ABS population and dwelling series show multi‑year gaps in selected states; capacity constraints and costs slowed delivery.

## Key Drivers
- Build cost inflation, labour availability, planning timelines, and net migration.

## What the Data Shows
- Approvals down from peaks; completions lumpy. Population inflows concentrated in a handful of LGAs.

## Expert Insight
- Watch leading indicators: approvals, project cancellations, builder insolvencies, and rent pressure.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: avoid over‑supply micro‑pockets; check pipeline at suburb level.
- Experienced: reconcile council approvals with likelihood of delivery; price in risk.

## Risks & Considerations
- Delivery risk, cost escalation, rate sensitivity of developers.

## Key Takeaways
- Shortages can be persistent locally even when headline supply rises.
- Due diligence must include pipeline and delivery probability.

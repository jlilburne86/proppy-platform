# Capital Cities vs Regional Markets: Where Growth Has Shifted

Post‑pandemic dynamics reshaped demand in both capitals and regionals. This guide compares the current signals and where momentum appears to be rotating.

![City vs regional signals](/assets/screenshots/platform-screenshot.png)
![Vacancy comparison](/assets/screenshots/technology.png)
![Listings and absorption](/assets/screenshots/how-it-works.png)
![Portfolio allocation view](/assets/screenshots/homepage-9.png)

## Market Context
- Regional outperformance narrowed as some capitals stabilised; conditions remain suburb‑specific within both groups.

## Key Drivers
- Migration patterns, remote/hybrid work normalising, transport upgrades, and relative affordability.

## What the Data Shows
- Selected capitals show tightening vacancy in inner/middle rings; regionals with constrained rental stock hold firm.

## Expert Insight
- Stock quality, amenities and commute patterns increasingly differentiate outcomes more than the city/regional label.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: compare like‑for‑like stock; avoid generalising from medians.
- Experienced: diversify across at least two cities/one regional where fundamentals fit strategy.

## Risks & Considerations
- Over‑extrapolating pandemic trends; supply surges in niche regional pockets.

## Key Takeaways
- Treat capitals and regionals as a spectrum; let indicators drive allocation.

Using Equity to Expand a Portfolio: An Educational Example

Equity can accelerate portfolio building if buffers are strong and serviceability remains sound. This walkthrough shows how to calculate usable equity, stress‚Äëtest cashflow, and sequence purchases safely.

Market Context

  • Rates: The RBA cash rate target was 3.60% on 10 Dec 2025 (RBA). Lender assessment rates remain higher than loan rates‚Äîbuild in realistic buffers.
  • Supply & rents: In many corridors, tight vacancy in 2025 supported rent stability, which helps servicing‚Äîbut treat rent assumptions conservatively (PropTrack).

Key Drivers

  • Vacancy: Lower vacancy supports rent growth and reduces leasing risk.
  • Rent momentum: Sustained rent increases improve gross yield and buffers.
  • Listings & DOM: Scarcer listings and falling DOM can confirm buyer depth.
  • Supply pipeline: Completions vs approvals inform medium‚Äëterm rental pressure.
  • Borrowing costs: Assessment rates shape buyer depth and turnover.
  • Stock mix: Houses vs units, and micro‚Äëlocation livability, drive dispersion.

What the Data Shows

  • Equity behaves like leverage: compounding works both ways. Sequence additions when multiple signals align and buffers are ample.
  • Rent stability plus controlled costs are more important than squeezing the last dollar of LVR.

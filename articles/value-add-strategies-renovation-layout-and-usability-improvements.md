# Value‑Add Strategies: Renovation, Layout and Usability Improvements

Simple changes in layout and usability can shift a property up a tenant demand tier. Here are common, evidence‑backed upgrades and where they make sense.

![Scope checklist](/assets/screenshots/how-it-works.png)
![Rent uplift tracking](/assets/screenshots/technology.png)
![Portfolio capex ledger](/assets/screenshots/homepage-9.png)

## Market Context
- In tight rental markets, small usability gains can drive outsized leasing outcomes.

## Key Drivers
- Tenant preferences (storage, light, heating/cooling), low‑maintenance finishes, and functional space.

## What the Data Shows
- Upgrades that reduce friction (laundry, storage, lighting) often rent fastest.

## Expert Insight
- Focus on ROI per dollar spent; avoid over‑capitalising for the submarket.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: start with a punch list of low‑risk, high‑impact items.
- Experienced: sequence larger works between tenancies.

## Risks & Considerations
- Cost overruns; scope creep; regulations for structural changes.

## Key Takeaways
- Value‑add should target tenant appeal and maintenance ease, not vanity metrics.

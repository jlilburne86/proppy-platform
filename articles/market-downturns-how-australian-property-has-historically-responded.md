## Market Context
- Financing costs: The RBA cash rate target was 3.60% on 10 Dec 2025 after earlier cuts in 2025 (RBA). Easing helps serviceability but doesn‚Äôt remove structural supply/demand constraints.
- Supply response: Approvals and commencements were below 2021 peaks into 2025 in many jurisdictions (ABS). Slow pipelines can cushion rentals in downturns.
- Demand signals: Vacancy and DOM typically adjust before price indexes; read them first and at suburb level (PropTrack/CoreLogic, 2024‚Äì2025).

## Key Drivers
- Vacancy: Lower vacancy supports rent growth and reduces leasing risk.
- Rent momentum: Sustained rent increases improve gross yield and buffers.
- Listings & DOM: Scarcer listings and falling DOM can confirm buyer depth.
- Supply pipeline: Completions vs approvals inform medium‚Äëterm rental pressure.
- Borrowing costs: Assessment rates shape buyer depth and turnover.
- Stock mix: Houses vs units, and micro‚Äëlocation livability, drive dispersion.

## What the Data Shows
- In slowdowns, listings often build first; price falls, if any, tend to be uneven and concentrated in over‚Äësupplied or lower‚Äëquality stock.
- Tight vacancy can limit rent declines even when transaction volumes soften; outer‚Äëring new‚Äëbuild pockets behave differently to inner‚Äëring established stock.
- Recovery phases are marked by stabilising listings and shorter DOM before prices broadly follow.

## Methodology & Criteria
- Dashboard (monthly/quarterly): Vacancy, rent YoY/QoQ, listings YoY, DOM, approvals, investor lending.
- Qualitative overlay: Micro‚Äëlocation (arterials/noise/flood), stock quality (layout, light, maintenance), body corporate/land size.
- Guideline thresholds: ‚ÄúVacancy < ~1.5% with rising rents‚Äù strengthens yield case; ‚ÄúDOM falling and listings tightening‚Äù strengthens growth case.

Worked examples
- Gross yield = annual rent / price. Example: $560/wk ‚Üí $29,120/yr on $620,000 ‚Üí 4.7% gross.
- Cashflow buffer (pre‚Äëtax) = annual rent ‚àí interest ‚àí insurance ‚àí rates ‚àí maintenance ‚àí PM fees. Stress test at +100‚Äì150 bps.
- Equity sensitivity (illustrative): +6% on $620,000 ‚Üí +$37,200 paper equity; compare to cashflow outcome.

## State Highlights (Qualitative)
- NSW/ACT: Inner‚Äëring scarcity dynamics; some middle‚Äëring units improve on yield where vacancy stays tight; outer‚Äëring depends on new‚Äëbuild delivery and commute realities.

## Risks & What to Watch Next
- Policy & Tenancy: Rule changes (notice periods, standards, short‚Äëstay caps) can influence leasing and costs.
- Supply shocks: If approvals/completions catch up quickly, rental tightness can ease; track ABS and local project updates.
- Migration & Employment: University and industry cycles shift tenant demand; review ABS and RBA commentary.
- Cost & Rates: Funding costs and assessment rates change buyer depth; monitor RBA decisions and lender pricing.

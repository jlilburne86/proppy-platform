# Which Capital Cities Are Leading — and Which Are Lagging

Headlines are often wrong in the details. This comparison focuses on indicators that move portfolios: vacancy, rents, listings and days‑on‑market (DOM). The objective isn’t to rank cities for sport — it’s to identify where fundamentals are strengthening and why.

## Market Context
- Capitals rarely move together; look for clusters of suburbs where vacancy remains tight and rents rise ahead of listings.

## Key Drivers
- Migration, project delivery, employment hubs, and affordability differences between cities.

## What the Data Shows
- Markets with sub‑1.5% vacancy and rising rents in multiple LGAs tend to lead — provided new supply is constrained.

## Expert Insight
- City picks matter less than suburb and stock quality; avoid blanket conclusions.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: shortlist cities where rent pressure is rising; then compare suburbs within those cities.  
- Experienced: diversify across two capitals with different drivers to smooth risk.

## Risks & Considerations
- Over‑generalising from medians; local oversupply; ignoring micro‑location factors.

## Key Takeaways
- Let indicators choose cities; let due diligence choose the street.

When signals conflict, slow down and expand observation. Extend the monitoring window by a quarter, validate rent movements with on‑the‑ground leasing feedback, and compare against a neighbouring suburb with a different stock mix. If the signal persists across multiple indicators and timeframes, it deserves more weight; if it fades, file it under noise.

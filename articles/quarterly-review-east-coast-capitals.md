# Quarterly Review: East Coast Capitals

What changed this quarter across Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane? We summarise the observable movements in listings, vacancy and rents — and outline what investors should monitor next.

## Market Context
- East coast conditions diverged; inventory is not moving in lockstep. Always compare suburb‑level indicators against city aggregates.

## Key Drivers
- Supply vs demand balance, migration inflows, and construction pipelines.
- Affordability and lending settings continue to shape buyer cohorts.

## What the Data Shows
- Sydney: listings increased in selected LGAs; absorption still healthy in tight rental pockets.
- Melbourne: rental markets tightened in inner/middle rings; watch time‑to‑lease and inspection volumes.
- Brisbane: SEQ corridors remain undersupplied; yields comparatively resilient in certain stock types.

## Expert Insight
- Focus on resilient demand drivers (employment nodes, transport access) rather than chasing headline medians.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: track one consistent set of indicators per city to avoid noise.
- Experienced: rotate attention across corridors; avoid over‑concentration in a single LGA.

## Risks & Considerations
- Policy changes (rental, planning), delivery slippage for new supply, and localized vacancy spikes.

## Key Takeaways
- Use suburb‑level data to avoid drawing the wrong conclusions from city aggregates.
- Maintain flexibility on stock type where fundamentals are strong.
- Re‑underwrite purchases when conditions move.

If the signal persists across multiple indicators and timeframes, it deserves more weight; if it fades, file it under noise.

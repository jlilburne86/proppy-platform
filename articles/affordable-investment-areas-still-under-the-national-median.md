# Affordable Investment Areas Still Under the National Median

“Affordable” should never mean “compromised”. This guide outlines how to find sub‑median price points with sound tenant demand, realistic rents and constrained supply — and how to avoid the traps that look cheap for a reason.

## Market Context
- Sub‑median opportunities exist in outer rings, smaller capitals and regionals — but dispersion is huge. Vacancy and pipeline, not the sticker price, determine value.

## Key Drivers
- Rent pressure (rents rising faster than listings), employment access, transport upgrades, and limited new land releases.

## What the Data Shows
- Areas with sub‑median prices plus sub‑1.5% vacancy and rising rents are rare but durable — especially where build capacity is tight.

## Expert Insight
- Avoid value traps: long DOM, abundant new supply, or poor tenant appeal (layout, light, parking). The discount isn’t worth the drag.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: buy the best property in the best street you can afford; don’t stretch for size over quality.  
- Experienced: pair a sub‑median asset (yield) with a constrained‑supply asset (growth) to balance portfolio risk.

## Risks & Considerations
- Estate oversupply, amenity lag, builder risk, and rental volatility in fringe pockets.

## Practical Checklist

- Vacancy <1.5% and rents rising.  
- Commute and employment access within acceptable thresholds.  
- Pipeline check: estates, approvals, and builder capacity.  
- Property quality: light, layout, parking, and low‑maintenance materials.

## Key Takeaways
- Sub‑median is only attractive when fundamentals are strong; price alone is never a thesis.

# Rental Market Pressures Explained: Supply, Demand and Rents

Rent outcomes reflect a handful of forces: vacancy, new supply, migration, and wage growth. Here’s how to read them.

![Vacancy map](/assets/screenshots/platform-screenshot.png)
![Rents trend](/assets/screenshots/technology.png)
![Listings & time-to-lease](/assets/screenshots/how-it-works.png)
![Portfolio rent roll](/assets/screenshots/homepage-9.png)

## Market Context
- Tight segments remain; others normalise as supply returns. Effects differ by stock type.

## Key Drivers
- Vacancy, migration patterns, wage growth, and new dwelling handovers.

## What the Data Shows
- Rent pressure tracks vacancy and stock mix; watch decile splits by submarket.

## Expert Insight
- Price to true demand; avoid assuming all stock rents equally fast.

## Investor Lens
- Beginners: verify local rent appraisals and comparable leases.
- Experienced: consider property upgrades that move tenants across quality tiers.

## Risks & Considerations
- Regulatory changes (caps/controls), sudden supply additions.

## Key Takeaways
- Track vacancy and stock mix; adjust pricing and expectations accordingly.
